## 1. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

### 1.1 Personal Information

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you register for an account, use our Services, contact us, or otherwise interact with our platform. Personal information may include:

**Identity Information:** Full name, username, title, company name, and other identifying information you provide during registration or profile creation.

**Contact Information:** Email address, phone number, mailing address, and other contact details necessary for communication and service delivery.

**Account Information:** Login credentials, account preferences, subscription details, and account activity information.

**Payment Information:** Billing address, payment method details, and transaction history. Note that we do not store complete credit card numbers or sensitive payment information on our servers; such information is processed by our certified payment processors.

**Professional Information:** Job title, company information, industry details, and other professional context relevant to your use of our AI assistant services.

**Communication Data:** Content of communications you send to us, including customer support inquiries, feedback, and other correspondence.

### 1.2 Voice and Audio Data

Given the nature of our AI assistant services, we collect and process voice and audio data:

**Voice Recordings:** Audio recordings of your interactions with our AI assistants, including voice commands, questions, and conversations during demo sessions or active use of our Services.

**Voice Transcripts:** Text transcriptions of your voice interactions, generated through speech-to-text processing for analysis and service improvement.

**Audio Metadata:** Technical information about audio recordings, including duration, quality metrics, and processing timestamps.

**Voice Biometric Data:** In some cases, voice pattern analysis may be performed to improve recognition accuracy and personalization, though we do not use voice data for identification purposes without explicit consent.

### 1.3 Usage and Technical Data

We automatically collect certain information when you access and use our Services:

**Device Information:** Information about the device you use to access our Services, including device type, operating system, browser type and version, screen resolution, and device identifiers.

**Usage Analytics:** Details about how you use our Services, including features accessed, session duration, interaction patterns, and performance metrics.

**Log Data:** Server logs that may include IP addresses, access times, pages viewed, time spent on pages, and other diagnostic data.

**Location Information:** General geographic location based on IP address, which helps us provide localized services and comply with regional regulations.

**Cookies and Tracking Technologies:** Information collected through cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies as described in our Cookie Policy.

### 1.4 AI Training and Model Data

To improve our AI assistant capabilities, we may collect:

**Interaction Patterns:** Anonymized data about how users interact with our AI assistants, including common queries, response effectiveness, and conversation flows.

**Performance Metrics:** Data about AI response accuracy, user satisfaction ratings, and system performance indicators.

**Feedback Data:** User feedback, ratings, and corrections provided to improve AI responses and capabilities.

**Behavioral Analytics:** Aggregated and anonymized patterns of user behavior that help us enhance our AI models and service offerings.

### 1.5 Third-Party Data

We may receive information about you from third-party sources:

**Integration Partners:** Data from business systems, CRM platforms, or other software that you connect to our Services with your authorization.

**Public Sources:** Publicly available information that helps us provide better service or verify account information.

**Referral Sources:** Information from users who refer you to our Services or from marketing partners with whom we have agreements.

**Right to Rectification:** You can request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information.

**Right to Erasure:** You can request deletion of your personal information in certain circumstances, such as when it's no longer necessary for the original purpose.

**Right to Restrict Processing:** You can request that we limit how we process your personal information in certain situations.

**Right to Data Portability:** You can request a copy of your personal information in a structured, machine-readable format.

**Right to Object:** You can object to certain types of processing, including processing for direct marketing purposes.

### 7.2 GDPR Rights (EEA and UK Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area or United Kingdom, you have additional rights under GDPR:

**Right to Withdraw Consent:** Where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw your consent at any time.

**Right to Lodge a Complaint:** You can file a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe we have violated your privacy rights.

**Right to Object to Automated Decision-Making:** You can object to decisions based solely on automated processing, including profiling.

### 7.3 CCPA Rights (California Residents)

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act:

**Right to Know:** You can request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect, use, disclose, and sell.

**Right to Delete:** You can request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions.

**Right to Opt-Out:** You can opt-out of the sale of your personal information (note that we do not sell personal information).

**Right to Non-Discrimination:** You have the right not to receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your privacy rights.

### 7.4 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the "Contact Us" section. We will respond to your request within the timeframes required by applicable law, typically within 30 days.
